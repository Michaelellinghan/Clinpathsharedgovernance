A Guide to Waste Management

Disposing of laboratory waste correctly is crucial for safety, compliance, and cost-effectiveness. Using the wrong bin can be surprisingly expensive and has a significant environmental impact.

Orange Clinical Waste Stream

This stream is for waste contaminated with blood or other bodily fluids that is not chemically contaminated and does not contain medicines. This waste is sent for alternative treatment (e.g., autoclaving) before disposal.

Approximate Cost per Bin: £15 - £25

  • Used sample tubes (EDTA, Serum, Heparin etc.)
  • Contaminated gloves and PPE
  • Blood product packaging
  • Used sample swabs
  • Contaminated bench roll

Yellow Clinical Waste Stream

This stream is for highly infectious waste or waste contaminated with certain chemicals or medicines. This waste must be sent for incineration, which is an energy-intensive and expensive process.

Approximate Cost per Bin: £40 - £60

  • Waste from isolation areas for certain high-risk infectious diseases.
  • Items contaminated with cytotoxic or cytostatic medicines.
  • Sharps contaminated with medicinal products.
  • Discarded medicinal vials.

Domestic / 'Black Bag' Waste

This is for general, non-clinical waste. Using this correctly for non-contaminated items is the most cost-effective and environmentally friendly option. It avoids unnecessary treatment or incineration.

Approximate Cost per Bin: £3 - £5

  • Paper towels and outer cardboard packaging
  • Non-contaminated stationery
  • Food wrappers and drink cans
  • Uncontaminated plastic packaging

The Bottom Line:

Placing a single non-infectious, non-contaminated item in a yellow stream bin means the whole bin must be incinerated at a high cost. Every item placed in the correct bin makes a significant financial and environmental difference. Think before you throw!
