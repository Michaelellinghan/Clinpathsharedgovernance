Massive Haemorrhage Protocol (MHP)

Our emergency response for critical bleeding.

Activation and Communication

The MHP is activated by a senior clinician when a patient's blood loss is life-threatening. This single action triggers an immediate, high-priority response from the transfusion lab. The first critical step for the Biomedical Scientist is to call the activating area, confirm patient identity, and ensure a clear line of communication is established for the duration of the event. All actions and component issues are meticulously recorded on the MHP Record Sheet to ensure a full audit trail.

MHP Pathways: A Tailored Response

Trauma Haemorrhage Pathway

For major trauma, the response is aggressive, using pre-defined packs with a balanced ratio of components to combat shock and coagulopathy early.

Pack 1

  • 4 units Red Cells (O Pos/Neg based on demographics)
  • 4 units FFP

Pack 2 & Beyond

  • 6 units Red Cells (Group Specific)
  • 4 units FFP
  • 1 unit Platelets
  • 2 units Cryoprecipitate (from Pack 3)

Obstetric Haemorrhage Pathway

The obstetric MHP is activated for severe postpartum haemorrhage. The response prioritises red cells and then uses targeted components based on lab results, especially Fibrinogen levels.

  • Initial Step: Issue 4-6 units of emergency (O Negative) or group-specific Red Cells immediately.
  • Second Step: Issue FFP and Cryoprecipitate. Cryoprecipitate is crucial for replacing Fibrinogen, which is often rapidly depleted in obstetric bleeds.
  • Further Support: Platelets and further components are guided by ongoing lab results.

Surgical/Medical Haemorrhage Pathway

For unexpected bleeds in theatre, recovery, or on the ward. This pathway is flexible and guided by the clinical team and lab results rather than fixed packs.

  • Initial Step: Provide 4-6 units of emergency or group-specific Red Cells.
  • Guided Therapy: Thaw and issue FFP, Cryoprecipitate, and Platelets only when requested by the clinical team, often after reviewing coagulation screen results. The Haematology registrar is a key point of contact for advising on component choice.

Cardiac Surgery Pathway

This pathway is used for catastrophic bleeding during or after cardiac surgery. It often involves point-of-care testing (ROTEM/TEG) alongside laboratory tests to guide very specific product use.

  • Initial components can be issued without haematology authorisation up to a limit (e.g., 4 RBC, 4 FFP, 2 Cryo, 2 Platelets).
  • Therapy is highly targeted to correct specific deficits shown on testing, for example, giving Cryoprecipitate to correct a low fibrinogen level.

Paediatric Haemorrhage Pathway

The paediatric MHP does not use fixed adult packs. All component volumes are carefully calculated based on the child's weight to provide life-saving treatment while avoiding fluid overload.

  • Red Cells / FFP: Typically dosed at 20 ml/kg.
  • Cryoprecipitate: Typically dosed at 10 ml/kg.
  • Emergency blood is always O Negative, CMV Negative, and Irradiated.
