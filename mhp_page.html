Massive Haemorrhage Protocol (MHP)

Our emergency response for critical bleeding.

Recognising a Massive Haemorrhage

A massive haemorrhage is a life-threatening medical emergency defined as the loss of 50% of a patient's blood volume within 3 hours, or a continuous bleeding rate of â‰¥150 ml/min. Early recognition and activation of the MHP is critical for patient survival and requires a coordinated, multidisciplinary approach.

The Team Response & Communication

When the MHP bleep is activated, it triggers an immediate response. This is a high-priority event that overrides all other routine work. Clear and concise communication is essential. The laboratory staff will confirm the patient's details and provide the clinical area with a dedicated MHP phone number (QMC 80552 / City 78067) to ensure a clear line of communication remains open. Every component issued is meticulously documented on the Massive Haemorrhage and Emergency Blood Record Sheet to ensure full traceability.

The MHP Packs: Tailored to the Situation

The contents of the MHP packs vary depending on the clinical situation, primarily distinguished by whether the bleed is due to trauma or is non-traumatic (e.g., obstetric or surgical).

Special Considerations

If a patient has known red cell antibodies, the laboratory must be informed immediately. While the initial emergency O Negative units are safe, finding compatible group-specific blood may take longer. The Haematology Registrar will be informed to help manage the clinical situation.
