The Full Blood Count (FBC)

A window into your health.

What We Measure

The FBC is performed on a sophisticated analyser that uses laser technology and electrical impedance to count and measure the different cells in your blood. It gives us a detailed report with many different parameters, but they mainly fall into three categories.

Red Cell Parameters

Haemoglobin (Hb): Measures the amount of oxygen-carrying protein in your red cells. Low levels indicate anaemia.
Red Blood Cell Count (RBC): The total number of red cells.
Haematocrit (Hct): The proportion of your blood that is made up of red cells.
Mean Cell Volume (MCV): The average size of your red cells. Small cells can suggest iron deficiency, while large cells can suggest a vitamin B12 or folate deficiency.
Mean Cell Haemoglobin (MCH): The average amount of haemoglobin inside each red cell.

White Cell Parameters

White Blood Cell Count (WBC): The total number of white cells. A high count often indicates infection.
Neutrophils: The most common type of white cell, your first line of defence against bacterial infections.
Lymphocytes: Key players in your immune response, fighting viral infections and remembering past invaders.
Monocytes: The 'clean-up crew' that engulfs dead cells and pathogens.
Eosinophils & Basophils: Specialised white cells involved in allergic reactions and fighting parasites.

Platelet Parameters

Platelet Count (Plt): The number of platelets in your blood. A low count can lead to bruising and bleeding, while a high count can increase the risk of clotting.
