Getting Samples to the Lab

A reliable result starts with a safe and timely journey. The way a sample travels to us can impact its stability and our turnaround times.

In-Hospital Transport

Pneumatic Tube System (POD)

This system is a convenient way to transport routine, non-time-critical samples to the laboratory. While direct, it is not the fastest method for urgent requests as pods can queue. The rapid transit can also agitate samples, so ensuring they are securely packaged is essential to prevent haemolysis or breakages. Time-critical samples must not be sent in the POD.

Portering Service

Our trusted porters perform scheduled and ad-hoc collections from wards and clinics. This is the primary method for transporting both routine and urgent samples, ensuring they are handled directly and delivered promptly to our reception.

Hand Delivery

For irreplaceable or clinically urgent / time-critical samples (e.g., major haemorrhage requests, CSFs, bone marrows), hand delivery is the safest and most reliable option. To ensure it is processed immediately, please hand the sample directly to a member of the Specimen Reception staff and state that it is urgent.

External Transport

GP Practices & Community Clinics

Samples from primary care are brought to the laboratory via dedicated van transport. These vans run on a scheduled timetable throughout the day, collecting from GP surgeries across the region. It is important that samples are packaged correctly and ready for the scheduled collection time to ensure they are processed within the expected 24-hour turnaround time.

Referral & External Hospitals

As a large teaching hospital, we act as a specialist testing centre for other NHS Trusts and healthcare providers. These samples arrive via courier or specialist transport and are often for complex, non-routine tests. They are logged and processed by our dedicated referrals team within Specimen Reception.
