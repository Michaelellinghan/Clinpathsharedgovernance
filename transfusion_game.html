How to Identify an Antibody

The Goal: To find an antibody specificity that matches the pattern of the patient's positive reactions.

  1. Rule Out (Exclusion): Look at the patient's negative reactions. If a panel cell is homozygous for an antigen (e.g., Jk(a+b-)) and the patient's plasma does not react with it, you can confidently rule out that antibody (in this case, Anti-Jka). You can click the antigen header on the panel to draw a line through it.
  2. Rule In (Pattern Matching): Look at the patient's positive reactions. The pattern of reactivity should match the antigen profile of a single antibody.
  3. Check the Auto-Control: The 'Auto' column shows the patient's plasma tested against their own cells. If this is positive, an auto-antibody may be present.
  4. Consider Dosage: Some antibodies react more weakly with heterozygous cells (e.g., K+k+) than homozygous cells (e.g., K+k-). This is called 'dosage'.

When you have identified the most likely antibody (or antibodies), select it from the dropdown and submit your answer!
