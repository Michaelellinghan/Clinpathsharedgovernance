The Group & Screen

The cornerstone of safe transfusion practice.

The First Step: The Right Sample

Before any analysis can begin, we must have a correctly collected and labelled blood sample. Patient identification is the single most critical step in ensuring transfusion safety. The sample must be a 6mL EDTA tube (pink top), and the label must contain four key identifiers: First Name, Last Name, Date of Birth, and the Hospital/NHS Number. The details on the sample must exactly match the details on the request form. Any discrepancy will lead to the sample being rejected.

The 'Group': What's Your Type?

The 'Group' determines a patient's ABO and RhD blood type. We do this in two ways simultaneously to ensure accuracy, a principle known as Landsteiner's Law.

  • Forward Group (Cell Grouping): We use manufactured antibodies (Anti-A, Anti-B, Anti-D) to test the patient's red blood cells for the presence of A, B, and D antigens on their surface.
  • Reverse Group (Plasma Grouping): We use manufactured A and B red cells to test the patient's plasma for the presence of Anti-A and Anti-B antibodies.

The results must be a perfect mirror image. For example, a Group A patient's red cells will react with Anti-A, and their plasma will react with B cells. The 'Screen': Looking for Unexpected Antibodies

The 'Screen' is a test to detect "atypical" red cell antibodies in the patient's plasma. These are antibodies other than the naturally occurring Anti-A and Anti-B. They are typically formed after a person is exposed to red cells different from their own, for example through a previous transfusion or pregnancy.

We test the patient's plasma against three different group O screening cells. These cells are specially chosen because, between them, they carry all the most common and clinically significant red cell antigens.

If the antibody screen is negative, it's highly likely the patient can receive a safe transfusion. If it's positive, we must perform further investigations to identify the antibody before any blood can be issued.
