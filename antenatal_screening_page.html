Antenatal Screening

Protecting the health of mother and baby through early testing.

Why do we screen in pregnancy?

Antenatal screening is a series of tests offered during pregnancy to identify any potential health problems that could affect you or your baby. By identifying these risks early, we can offer information, support, and treatment to give your baby the best possible start in life. All screening is optional, and your midwife will discuss all the tests with you.

Our Antenatal Screening Tests

Blood Group & Antibody Screen

At your booking appointment, we test your blood to find out your ABO and RhD blood group. This is vital in case you need a blood transfusion. We also check for any unusual blood group antibodies that could potentially cross the placenta and affect the baby's red blood cells, a condition called Haemolytic Disease of the Fetus and Newborn (HDFN).

For RhD Negative Mums: Anti-D

If you are RhD negative, you'll be offered injections of a substance called Anti-D immunoglobulin during pregnancy and after birth if your baby is RhD positive. This prevents your immune system from producing antibodies that could be harmful in future pregnancies. We can also now offer a test to predict your baby's D type from your own blood sample.

Sickle Cell & Thalassaemia Screening

All pregnant women are offered screening for these inherited blood disorders. If the mother is found to be a carrier, we then offer a test to the baby's biological father. This allows us to determine the chance of the baby inheriting a serious condition. You can find out more on our Specialist Haematology page.

Infectious Diseases

We also offer screening for Hepatitis B, HIV, and Syphilis. These are infections that can be passed from mother to baby. Finding them early means we can offer treatment and care that significantly reduces the risk of transmission, protecting your baby's health.
