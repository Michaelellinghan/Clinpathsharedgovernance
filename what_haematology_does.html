Understanding Haematology

We're the blood detectives, solving medical mysteries one cell at a time.

What is Haematology?

Haematology is the branch of medicine dedicated to the study of blood, blood-forming organs, and blood-related diseases. When your doctor takes a blood sample, our team of 'blood detectives' analyses it to find crucial clues about your health, from diagnosing anaemia to identifying serious conditions like leukaemia.

The Journey of a Haematology Sample

  1. Step 1: Arrival & Booking In

    The journey begins when a blood sample, typically in a purple-top tube, arrives in Specimen Reception and is booked into our system.

  2. Step 2: The Full Blood Count (FBC)

    The sample's first stop is our automated analyser for an FBC, which counts and measures all the different types of blood cells, giving a vital snapshot of the patient's health.

  3. Step 3: Blood Film Morphology

    If the FBC results are abnormal, a blood film is made. A skilled scientist examines the cells under a microscope, looking for visual clues that the machines can't see.

  4. Step 4: Specialist Testing

    Depending on the clinical details or initial findings, the sample may proceed to more specialist tests, such as coagulation studies or investigations for inherited red cell disorders.
