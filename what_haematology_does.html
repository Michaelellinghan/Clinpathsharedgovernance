The Journey of an FBC Sample

  1. Step 1: Arrival & Automated Sorting

    The journey begins when a blood sample arrives. It's booked into our system and placed on an automated track which sorts it for analysis.

  2. Step 2: The Full Blood Count (FBC)

    The sample's first stop is a high-tech analyser for a Full Blood Count. This machine counts and measures all the different blood cells, giving a vital snapshot of the patient's health.

  3. Step 3: Scientific Validation

    The results from the analyser are reviewed by a skilled Biomedical Scientist, checking for any flags or abnormalities that may require a closer look.

  4. Step 4: The Blood Film Review

    If the FBC is abnormal, a thin film of blood is made and stained. A scientist then examines the cells under a microscope, looking for visual clues that the machines can't see. This is the only test that is a direct follow-up from the FBC.

Other tests like Coagulation screens or Malaria checks are separate pathways, requested specifically by clinicians based on the patient's condition.
