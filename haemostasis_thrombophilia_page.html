Investigating the complex world of inherited and acquired bleeding and clotting disorders.

Investigating a Bleeding Tendency

When a patient has an unexplained prolonged clotting time (usually APTT), we perform mixing studies to determine if it's due to a factor deficiency or an inhibitor.

Mixing Studies

We mix the patient's plasma 1:1 with normal plasma (which contains 100% of all factors) and repeat the prolonged test.
- If the result corrects into the normal range, it suggests the patient is deficient in a clotting factor.
- If the result fails to correct, it suggests the patient's plasma contains an inhibitor (like a lupus anticoagulant or a specific factor inhibitor) that is acting on the normal plasma too.

Investigating a Clotting Tendency (Thrombophilia)

Thrombophilia is a predisposition to thrombosis (blood clots). We perform a panel of tests to look for common inherited and acquired causes.

Lupus Anticoagulant
