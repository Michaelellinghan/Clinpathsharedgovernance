Routine Coagulation Tests

Exploring the workhorse tests of the coagulation laboratory, what they measure, and how we interpret them.

Inside the Coagulation Lab

We use automated coagulation analysers (like the Sysmex CS-series) to perform our tests. These instruments use sophisticated methods to detect clot formation in patient plasma after the addition of specific activating reagents.

The Optical Detection Method

This is the primary method for most of our tests (PT, APTT, Fibrinogen). The analyser shines a light beam through the cuvette containing the patient's plasma and the test reagent. As a fibrin clot forms, the plasma becomes more cloudy or opaque. This causes the amount of light passing through the cuvette to decrease. The analyser measures this change in light transmission, and the time taken from the addition of the reagent to the formation of the clot is recorded as the clotting time in seconds.

Immuno-turbidimetric Methods

For D-Dimer, we use an immuno-turbidimetric assay. This uses latex particles coated with an antibody specific to the D-Dimer fragment. When D-Dimer is present in the plasma, it causes the particles to agglutinate (clump together). This clumping increases the turbidity of the sample, which is measured by a change in light transmission. The degree of change is proportional to the concentration of D-Dimer.

Prothrombin Time (PT)13.5s

Prothrombin Time

Measures the extrinsic and common pathways. Prolonged in liver disease, vitamin K deficiency, and DIC. The basis for the INR.

INR1.0

International Normalised Ratio

A standardised calculation of the PT, used to monitor patients on Warfarin therapy. Therapeutic ranges are typically 2.0-3.0 or 3.0-4.0 depending on the clinical indication.

APTT32s

Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time

Measures the intrinsic and common pathways. Prolonged in haemophilia A and B, von Willebrand disease, and with heparin therapy.

Fibrinogen3.5g/L

Fibrinogen

The precursor to fibrin, the final component of a clot. It is an acute phase protein, so is raised in inflammation. Low levels are seen in DIC and severe liver disease.

D-Dimer200ng/mL

D-Dimer

A specific breakdown product of a fibrin clot. A high level indicates that clotting and subsequent fibrinolysis has occurred. Used to help exclude VTE (DVT/PE). A negative result has a high negative predictive value.
