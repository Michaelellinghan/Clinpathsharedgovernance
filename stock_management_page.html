Blood Stock Management

A precious resource, carefully managed.

Balancing Supply and Demand

Managing our inventory of blood components is a critical daily task. We need to have enough stock on the shelves to meet the demands of routine surgeries and unexpected emergencies, while also minimizing wastage of these precious, life-saving products. All our blood components are supplied by NHS Blood and Transplant (NHSBT).

Our Stock Management Strategy

Optimal Stock Levels

We have carefully calculated optimal stock levels for each blood component, based on historical usage and the clinical needs of our hospitals. We monitor these levels daily and place orders with NHSBT to replenish our stocks as needed. We receive routine deliveries twice a day on weekdays and once a day on weekends.

Stock Rotation

Blood components have a limited shelf life (e.g., 35 days for red cells, 7 days for platelets). To minimize wastage, we operate a strict "first in, first out" system. The oldest units are always selected first for transfusion. Units nearing their expiry date are flagged, and we make every effort to use them before they expire, sometimes by transferring them between our two hospital sites to where they are most needed.

Emergency Supply

For urgent or rare requirements that we cannot meet from our own stock, we can place ad-hoc or "blue light" emergency orders with NHSBT, who can deliver to us within a couple of hours. This ensures we can always get the right blood for any patient, no matter their requirements.

Traceability

Every single blood component has a unique donation number. We have a robust "vein-to-vein" traceability system, meaning we can track every unit from the donor, through our laboratory, to the final recipient. This is a legal requirement and a cornerstone of blood safety.
