Blood Stock Management

A precious resource, carefully managed.

The Core Principles of Stock Control

Optimal Levels & Ordering

We maintain carefully calculated stock levels to balance patient demand with the risk of wastage. Daily stock counts are performed and orders are placed with NHSBT via the Online Blood Ordering System (OBOS) to replenish stocks for routine deliveries.

Strict Stock Rotation

To prevent expiry, we operate a "first in, first out" system. However, we also use logic: the oldest units are selected first for top-up transfusions, while units with a longer shelf-life are chosen for surgical cover where blood may be returned unused.

The Cold Chain

All red cell components must be kept within a tightly controlled temperature range of 2-6Â°C. We ensure this "cold chain" integrity is maintained from the point of donation to the moment of transfusion to guarantee the safety and efficacy of the blood.

Vein-to-Vein Traceability

It is a mandatory requirement to ensure full traceability of every blood component. We must know the location and final fate of every unit, whether it is transfused, transferred to another hospital, or wasted for any reason.

Minimising Wastage: A Precious Resource

Blood is a precious gift, and we have a responsibility to minimise wastage. Any unit that is unsuitable for transfusion must be disposed of and recorded electronically to maintain the audit trail.
