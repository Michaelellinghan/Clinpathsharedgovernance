The Pre-Analytical Partnership: Help Us Get It Right First Time

The pre-analytical phase—everything that happens before the sample reaches the analyser—is where most errors occur. By following these best practices, you can significantly reduce delays and improve patient safety.

1. Perfect Labelling: The Most Critical Step

Incorrect labelling is the single biggest cause of sample rejection and delays. Our analysers rely entirely on the barcode for identification. Please ensure every label is:

  • Straight and Vertical: Applied directly over the manufacturer's label. Crooked labels cannot be read by the automation.
  • One Per Tube: Never apply multiple labels. Add-on tests should be requested via ICE.
  • Clear and High Quality: Faded or smudged barcodes will fail. Please check your printer's ink levels.
  • Correctly Placed: Labels must not overhang the top or bottom of the tube, as this can cause jams and sample loss on the track.

2. The Right Tube for the Right Test

The colour of a tube's lid indicates the specific anticoagulant or additive inside, which is vital for preserving the sample correctly for the requested test. Using the wrong tube will mean the test cannot be performed and a re-bleed will be required.

  • EDTA (Purple): Used for haematology tests (e.g., FBC) as it preserves cell morphology.
  • Citrate (Light Blue): Essential for coagulation tests as the anticoagulant is reversible. The tube **must** be filled to the line.
  • Serum SST (Gold/Yellow): Contains a gel separator and clot activator, used for most chemistry tests.
  • Fluoride Oxalate (Grey): Prevents glycolysis, essential for accurate glucose and lactate measurements.
