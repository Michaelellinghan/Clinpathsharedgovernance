Crossmatch & Issue

The final, critical safety checks before transfusion.

The Two Paths to a Safe Transfusion

Once a patient's blood group is known and their antibody screen is complete, we perform a final compatibility test between the patient and the donor blood unit. This can be done in two ways: a modern, computer-based "Electronic Issue" or a traditional, hands-on "Serological Crossmatch". The path we take is strictly determined by the patient's history and test results to ensure maximum safety.

Electronic Issue (EI)

Electronic Issue is a "virtual" crossmatch performed by our laboratory computer system. It is a safe and efficient method that allows us to get blood to patients quickly. However, a patient must meet strict criteria to be eligible.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • The patient must have a current negative antibody screen.
  • The patient must have no history of clinically significant antibodies.
  • The patient's blood group must be fully validated on our system, often from two separate samples taken at different times.

If all rules are met, the computer allows us to select compatible units and release them.

Full Serological Crossmatch

If a patient is not eligible for EI, we must perform a traditional "wet" crossmatch. This is a physical test to prove compatibility.

The Process:

  1. We mix a sample of the patient's plasma with red cells taken directly from the donor blood bag.
  2. The mixture is incubated at 37Â°C in a special gel card.
  3. After incubation, the card is centrifuged and examined for agglutination (clumping).
  4. No agglutination means the unit is compatible and safe to issue.
  5. Any agglutination indicates an incompatibility. The unit must not be issued, and further investigation is required.

The Final Release: Last Checks for Safety

Regardless of the crossmatch method used, a series of critical final checks must be completed before any blood component leaves the laboratory. This is the last chance to catch any potential errors.

  • We check that the patient's details and blood group on the request form, our computer system, and the compatibility label are a perfect match.
  • We confirm that all special requirements, such as irradiated or CMV negative blood, have been met.
  • We visually inspect the blood bag for any leaks, clots, or unusual colour that could indicate bacterial contamination.
  • Once all checks are complete, the unit is placed in a dedicated, temperature-controlled issue fridge, ready for collection.
  • Staff collecting the blood must use the Blood360 electronic tracking system, often with a biometric fingerprint scan, to ensure only trained personnel can access the fridge and that the correct unit is matched to the correct patient at the point of collection.
