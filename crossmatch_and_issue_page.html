Crossmatch & Issue

The final, critical safety check before transfusion.

The Last Step: Ensuring Compatibility

Once a patient's group and screen is complete, and blood has been requested, the final step is to issue compatible units. This process can be done in two ways: a traditional serological crossmatch or a modern electronic issue. The method we use depends on the patient's transfusion history and antibody status.

Methods of Issue

Full Serological Crossmatch

This is the traditional "wet" crossmatch. We physically mix the patient's plasma with red cells from the actual donor unit that we intend to issue. We incubate this mixture at 37Â°C and then check for agglutination (clumping).

If there is no agglutination, the blood is compatible and safe to issue.

We must perform a full serological crossmatch if:

  • The patient has a currently positive antibody screen.
  • The patient has a history of clinically significant antibodies.
  • There are any discrepancies or uncertainties in the patient's record.

Electronic Issue (EI)

Electronic issue is a "virtual" crossmatch performed by our laboratory information system (LIMS). Instead of physically mixing samples, the computer checks a series of rules to ensure safety.

To be eligible for electronic issue, a patient must:

  • Have a current negative antibody screen.
  • Have no history of clinically significant antibodies.
  • Have a validated blood group on record (usually from two separate samples).

If all the rules are met, the computer allows us to select compatible units from our stock and issue them without a wet crossmatch. Electronic issue is safe, fast, and efficient, allowing us to get blood to patients quickly.
