Further Haematology Tests

Exploring key markers of inflammation, infection, and specialised fluid analysis.

Plasma Viscosity (PV)

The PV is a reliable and stable measure of the acute phase response. It measures the thickness (viscosity) of blood plasma, which increases with the amount of plasma proteins, such as fibrinogen and immunoglobulins. It is generally preferred over the ESR as it is not affected by anaemia.

Sample: 4mL EDTA (Purple Top)

Use: Primarily for monitoring inflammatory conditions like Temporal Arteritis and Polymyalgia Rheumatica.

Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)

The ESR is a traditional, non-specific marker of inflammation. It measures the rate at which red blood cells settle in a vertical tube over one hour. The rate is increased when inflammatory proteins coat the red cells, causing them to fall faster. However, the result can be affected by factors other than inflammation, such as anaemia.

Sample: 4mL EDTA (Purple Top)

Use: Largely superseded by PV, but still used in some specific clinical scenarios.

Glandular Fever Screen (IM)

Also known as the Monospot test, this is a rapid screen for heterophile antibodies produced in response to an Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection. A blood film review is always performed alongside this test to look for the characteristic 'atypical lymphocytes' which support the diagnosis.

Sample: 4mL EDTA (Purple Top)

Note: False negatives can occur, especially in the early stages of the illness.

CSF Cytospin

This is a specialised technique used to concentrate cells from Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) onto a microscope slide. This allows for detailed morphological examination to identify cells that are present in very low numbers, which is crucial for diagnosing conditions like meningitis, subarachnoid haemorrhage, or the spread of cancer to the central nervous system.

Sample: Fresh Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) in a sterile universal container.
