Further Haematology Tests

Key investigations beyond the FBC and blood film.

Malaria Screening

When a doctor suspects malaria, we perform an urgent microscopic examination of a blood film. This is a time-critical investigation where our scientists meticulously search for parasites within the patient's red blood cells. A rapid diagnosis is crucial for starting life-saving treatment.

Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)

The ESR is a non-specific marker of inflammation. The test measures how quickly red blood cells settle in a tube of blood. A faster rate can indicate inflammation and is useful for monitoring conditions like rheumatoid arthritis or temporal arteritis.

Infectious Mononucleosis (IM) Screen

Also known as glandular fever, this test looks for specific antibodies that the body produces in response to the Epstein-Barr virus. A positive result helps to confirm the diagnosis, especially when combined with typical symptoms and blood film findings.

Polycythaemia Vera (PV) Investigation

When a patient has a persistently high red blood cell count, we can perform molecular testing to look for a specific gene mutation (JAK2 V617F). This mutation is found in over 95% of cases of Polycythaemia Vera, a condition that causes the blood to become too thick.
