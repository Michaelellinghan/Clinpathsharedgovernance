What is the Blood Transfusion Lab?

The Blood Transfusion department is the vital link between a blood donation and a patient receiving a life-saving transfusion. Our primary role is to ensure that every bag of blood or blood component is safe and, most importantly, compatible with the patient who needs it.

We perform a series of critical tests to match donated blood with a patient's own blood type. This process, called crossmatching, prevents dangerous transfusion reactions. We're on call 24/7, ready to respond to routine requests for surgery and to life-or-death emergencies where every second counts.

In an Emergency: The Massive Haemorrhage Protocol

When a patient is bleeding heavily, there's no time for full crossmatching. The "Massive Haemorrhage" bleep activates an immediate response from our team. We provide O RhD Negative red cells, the universal donor group, straight from a special fridge, along with other components like FFP in pre-prepared packs. This allows life-saving treatment to begin while we work on providing group-specific blood as soon as we can.
