What is the Blood Transfusion Lab?

The Blood Transfusion department is the vital link between a blood donation and a patient receiving a life-saving transfusion. Our primary role is to ensure that every unit of blood or blood component is safe and, most importantly, compatible with the patient who needs it. We are on call 24/7 to respond to both routine surgical requests and life-or-death emergencies where every second counts.

The Journey of a Transfusion Request

  1. Step 1: Request & Sample Arrival

    The process begins when a doctor requests blood and a correctly labelled blood sample arrives in the laboratory.

  2. Step 2: Group & Screen

    We perform the foundational test to determine the patient's blood group (ABO/RhD) and screen for any pre-existing red cell antibodies.

  3. Step 3: Antibody Investigation

    If the screen is positive, a detailed investigation begins to identify the specific antibody, ensuring we can select blood that is safe for the patient.

  4. Step 4: Crossmatch

    A final compatibility test is performed, either electronically or manually, to ensure the chosen donor unit will not react with the patient's plasma.

  5. Step 5: Safe Issue

    After final checks, the blood is placed in a special issue fridge, ready for collection and transfusion to the patient.
