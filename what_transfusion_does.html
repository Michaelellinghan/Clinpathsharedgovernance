Blood Transfusion: The Gift of Life

Ensuring the right blood gets to the right patient at the right time.

What is the Blood Transfusion Lab?

The Blood Transfusion department is the vital link between a blood donation and a patient receiving a life-saving transfusion. Our primary role is to ensure that every bag of blood or blood component is safe and, most importantly, compatible with the patient who needs it.

We perform a series of critical tests to match donated blood with a patient's own blood type. This process, called crossmatching, prevents dangerous transfusion reactions. We're on call 24/7, ready to respond to routine requests for surgery and to life-or-death emergencies where every second counts.

The Group & Screen: Our Core Safety Test

Before any transfusion, we must be absolutely certain the donor's blood won't be rejected by the patient's body. The 'Group and Screen' is the first step.

1. The 'Group'

We determine the patient's ABO and RhD blood group. We test the patient's red cells to see what antigens are on their surface, and we test their plasma to see which ABO antibodies they have. The two tests must match for the group to be valid - this is called Landsteiner's Law.

2. The 'Screen'

We screen the patient's plasma for any pre-existing antibodies against other, less common, blood group antigens. These antibodies could have been developed from previous transfusions or pregnancies. If this screen is positive, we must perform detailed investigations to identify the antibody before we can find compatible blood.

More Than Just Red Cells

A single blood donation can be separated into several different parts, or components. This allows us to give patients only the specific part of the blood they need.

Red Blood Cells

Used to treat anaemia or to replace blood lost during surgery or trauma. They carry oxygen around the body.

Platelets

Given to patients with low platelet counts to prevent or treat bleeding. Essential for blood clotting.

Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)

The liquid part of the blood, rich in clotting factors. Used for patients with complex clotting problems or after massive blood loss.

Cryoprecipitate

A concentrated source of certain clotting factors, especially Fibrinogen. Used to treat specific bleeding disorders or in major haemorrhage.

In an Emergency: The Massive Haemorrhage Protocol

When a patient is bleeding heavily, there's no time for full crossmatching. The "Massive Haemorrhage" bleep activates an immediate response from our team. We provide O RhD Negative red cells, the universal donor group, straight from a special fridge, along with other components like FFP in pre-prepared packs. This allows life-saving treatment to begin while we work on providing group-specific blood as soon as we can.
