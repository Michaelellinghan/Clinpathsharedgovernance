Malaria Screening

A time-critical hunt for blood parasites.

Urgent Microscopic Examination

Malaria is a serious tropical disease caused by a parasite that infects red blood cells. When a doctor suspects malaria, usually in a patient with a fever and a history of foreign travel, they will request an urgent malaria screen.

This is a manual microscopy test. We prepare two types of blood film:

  • A thick film, which allows us to screen a larger volume of blood to detect the presence of parasites.
  • A thin film, which is used to identify the specific species of malaria parasite and determine the percentage of red cells that are infected.

This is a time-critical investigation, as a rapid diagnosis allows for prompt, life-saving treatment. Biomedical Scientists performing this test must demonstrate excellent microscopic skills to find and identify the parasites.
