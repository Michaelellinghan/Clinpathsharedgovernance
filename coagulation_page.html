The Science of Haemostasis

Haemostasis is the body's process for stopping bleeding. It's a delicate balance between pro-coagulant (clot-forming) and anti-coagulant/fibrinolytic (clot-busting) systems. Our job is to investigate when this balance goes wrong.

1. Vascular Spasm

The damaged blood vessel constricts to reduce blood flow.

2. Platelet Plug

Platelets adhere to the injury site and activate, forming a temporary plug.

3. Coagulation

The cascade of clotting factors is activated, leading to the formation of a stable fibrin clot.

4. Fibrinolysis

Once the vessel has healed, the clot is broken down to restore normal blood flow.
