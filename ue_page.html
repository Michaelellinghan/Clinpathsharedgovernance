Basic Chemistry (U&E's)

These are some of the most common tests performed in sequence on our automated analysers, giving a speedy and concise view of a patient's renal function.

What We Test

The humble U&E is a collection of tests for sodium, potassium, urea, and creatinine. We also calculate an eGFR (estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate).

Once a sample is spun and the serum is separated from the red cells, these tests can be added on for up to 3 days.

How We Test

  • Potassium and Sodium rely on indirect Ion Selective Electrode (ISE) methods.
  • Urea relies on kinetic urease reactions.
  • Creatinine uses Compensated Kinetic Jaffe reactions.
  • Our automated lab procedures allow these tests to be performed quickly, cutting costs in time and resources and enabling a high throughput of samples.

Process Pathway

Booking In: ~25 minutes
Routine samples are booked in batches, with each sample taking a minute or two for quality checks.

Processing: ~16 minutes
This includes loading, centrifugation (spinning), and sending to the correct destination.

Analysis: ~7 minutes
Because of automation, analysis times are not cumulative. A normal result is sent as soon as it's complete.

Total Run Time (start to finish): ~50 minutes
